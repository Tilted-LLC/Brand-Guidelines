Introduction

Who we are

Tilted LLC is a development studio that builds and ships. Our brand reflects how we work — precise, direct, and unapologetically technical. Every element of this identity is rooted in the tools and culture of the people we build for and with.

// Personality
Direct. Sharp. Builders, not talkers. We do things a little sideways — and that's the point.
// Audience
Technical founders, CTOs, product teams who want a partner that speaks their language.
// Tone
Confident without being loud. Technically fluent. No buzzwords, no fluff. Ship it.

Color

Color system

Five tokens. Navy is the foundation — deep, authoritative, dark-mode native. Orange is the signal — every time it appears, it means something. Use it sparingly and it earns its weight.

✓ Do
  • Use navy as the dominant background on dark layouts
  • Apply orange only to the ❯ prompt, cursor, and key accent moments
  • Use muted (#8888AA) for the ./ separator
  • Keep white text on navy for maximum contrast
✗ Don't
  • Don't use orange as a background color at large scale
  • Don't introduce new brand colors outside this system
  • Don't use orange text on white backgrounds
  • Don't render the full mark on a busy or patterned background

Usage

Do's and don'ts

The mark works hard. Keep it clean and let it do its job — don't decorate it, don't crowd it, don't dilute it.

✓ Do
  • Use the full mark ❯./tilted on dark backgrounds wherever possible
  • Use the compact mark ./tilted in plain text contexts
  • Apply the cursor block in digital/animated contexts
  • Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the mark on all sides
  • Use Fira Code 700 for all logo renderings
  • Keep the ❯ and ./ in their designated colors
✗ Don't
  • Don't stretch, skew, rotate, or distort the mark
  • Don't change the font to anything other than Fira Code
  • Don't use the mark at sizes below 16px where detail is lost
  • Don't apply drop shadows, gradients, or outlines to the mark
  • Don't place the mark on a busy photo or patterned background
  • Don't rearrange the order of elements — chevron always leads
Voice & Copy

How we write

Our voice matches our mark — precise, no wasted characters. Write like you're writing code: clear intent, no bloat, and it should actually run when you're done.

❯ Direct & declarative
State what you do. Skip the qualifiers. "We build products" not "We help companies achieve their digital transformation goals."
❯ Technical but not exclusive
Use technical language when it's the right tool. Don't over-explain to technical audiences, but don't alienate business stakeholders either.
❯ Ship-oriented
We talk about what's been built and what's being built. Not about process for its own sake. Outcomes over methodology.
❯ Dry humor, earned
A bit of wit is on brand. Status bar copy like "exit code: 0" or "branch: main" — that kind of thing. Never try-hard, never cringe.
✓ Sounds like us
"We build the thing. Then we ship it."

"Software that works the first time takes longer to spec, not longer to build."

"./tilted — run it."
✗ Doesn't sound like us
"We leverage cutting-edge technology to deliver bespoke digital solutions."

"Your success is our passion."

"Synergizing innovation with agile frameworks."
